Biography

Thomas Alva Edison (1847–1931) was an American inventor, scientist, and businessman. He is best known for his invention of the phonograph, the incandescent light bulb, and the development of motion pictures. Over his lifetime, Edison accumulated 1,093 patents for his inventions, making him one of the most prolific inventors in history.

Famous Inventions

  • Incandescent Light Bulb: One of Edison’s most famous inventions, the light bulb revolutionized how people live, work, and socialize, making electric lighting widespread.
  • Phonograph: Edison’s phonograph was the first machine capable of recording and reproducing sound, a groundbreaking development for the music industry.
  • Motion Pictures: Edison developed the first motion picture studio and was a key figure in the development of early film technology, leading to the creation of the modern film industry.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1847: Thomas Edison is born in Milan, Ohio, USA.
  • 1877: Edison invents the phonograph, his first major invention.
  • 1879: Edison invents the practical incandescent light bulb.
  • 1887: Edison opens his first motion picture studio in West Orange, New Jersey.
  • 1931: Thomas Edison passes away, leaving behind a legacy of innovation.

Did You Know?

  • Thomas Edison was homeschooled after being considered a slow learner as a child.
  • He set up his first laboratory at the age of 10 in a small building near his home.
  • Despite his success, Edison was known for his relentless work ethic and belief in hard work over formal education.
